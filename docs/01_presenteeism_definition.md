# Presenteeism Definition

In this study, presenteeism means:

> A state in which a person continues participating in work, school, or team activity while experiencing physical, mental, or health-related difficulty, without being absent, leaving, or withdrawing.

This definition deliberately does not reduce presenteeism to laziness, poor concentration, low productivity, or insufficient effort.

## Why It Matters For Security And Privacy

Presenteeism is security/privacy-relevant because the person is still present, visible, and participating, while their difficulty may be sensitive and costly to disclose. A support system introduced in this context can create new information flows across power and evaluation relationships.

The key risk structure is:

1. The person is unwell or not fully well.
2. The person has not left or been absent.
3. Others may see ordinary participation.
4. Disclosure can carry evaluation, relational, or organizational cost.
5. A support system senses, logs, infers, or visualizes state-related information.
6. The support system can become surveillance, assessment, or labeling.

Paper 1A handles presenteeism as a context for evaluating support-system risk. It does not measure whether a participant is actually experiencing presenteeism during the experiment.
